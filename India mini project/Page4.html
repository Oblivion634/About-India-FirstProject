India being the second largest country in terms of world's population, it obviously tends to have the maximum number of individuals in its country, accounting for the varied different age groups of the people.

India's literacy rate has shown significant improvement over the years, reflecting the country's commitment to education. With concerted efforts and various initiatives, India has made substantial strides in reducing illiteracy.

As of September 2021, the literacy rate in India stood at around 77.7%. While this figure demonstrates progress, challenges still exist, particularly in rural areas and among marginalized communities. The Indian government has implemented numerous programs to address these gaps, including the Sarva Shiksha Abhiyan and the National Literacy Mission, which aim to provide universal access to quality education. Additionally, advancements in technology and digital literacy initiatives are further contributing to improving the literacy landscape across the country.

India's ongoing commitment to literacy highlights its recognition of education as a powerful tool for individual empowerment and national development.
